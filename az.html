Two Datasets for Argumentative Zoning of Scientific Documents

The AZ abstract corpus contains 1k PubMed abstracts from years 1998 to 2008 from journals representing different areas of biomedicine (e.g. molecular biology, epidemiology, toxicology, etc.). Each sentence in the abstracts has been annotated with one of the following labels: Background, Objective, Method, Result, Conclusion, Related work, and Future work, supporting the task of argumentative zoning (AZ). The corpus is distributed in both html and txt files. If you use the corpus in published work, please cite the following paper:

Guo Y, Korhonen A, Liakata M, Karolinska IS, Sun L, Stenius U. 2010. Identifying the information structure of scientific abstracts: an investigation of three different schemes. In Proceedings of the 2010 Workshop on Biomedical Natural Language Processing. [pdf]

The AZ article corpus contains 50 full-text articles from journals frequently used in cancer risk assessment. Each sentence in the articles has been annotated with one of the following labels: Background, Problem, Method, Result, Conclusion, Connection, Difference, and Future work, supporting the task of AZ. The corpus is distributed in both html and txt files. If you use the corpus in published work, please cite the following paper:

Guo Y, Silins I, Stenius U, Korhonen A. 2013. Active learning-based information structure analysis of full scientific articles and two applications for biomedical literature review. Bioinformatics, 29(11):1440-7. [pdf]
